FAQ

Click on a topic below.

Why did you decide to become a web-dev?

There are many things that have lead me to where I am today. In short I decided I wanted to pursue an incredible skill-set over pursuing typical business/management hierarchy. Having a skill that people need, that I can grow, that can truly impact a business is my number one desire. This skillset in particular is needed worldwide and is interesting. I am a techy true and true. A part of me also loves creating visually. So when you think about it, web-development is really prime for what I want to do in my day-to-day life... I am also a hipster so yeah.

What is your design process?

The design process is possibly the most important aspect of web application building, websites, and anything that users will be utilizing. It needs to be clean, functional, and relevant. That being said I tend to start from chaos. I like to play with different options on simple layouts and build from there. One important aspect to me is enveloping my style into the design. I love following great design trends, and I love even more incorporating myself into them.

What is your ultimate goal?

My ultimate goal is simple. To acquire and fine tune a great skillset. I want the freedom to do my work anywhere, and utilize an ever growing set of knowledge to create beautiful things for people to see and use! I am a learner over all and have high expectations of myself to be completely plugged in to the tech world.

Would you rather do Freelance or work for a business?

This is a question I have heard the influences in my life being asked quite a bit. I am not sure which I would prefer. I think that would depend on the experiences I have in each environment. I do know that I love a creative work space which in Oklahoma tends to come in very small doses. That being said, having a dedicated place to go and do your work, with surrounding personel that can help and encourage, as well as providing a very creative work place is the ideal work environment for me.

Who are some people who influenced your decision to pursue this career path?

I have always been "interested" in web development and design, however it wasn't until watching some YouTube videos of Travis Nielson that I felt truly inspired to dive head first and deep into this world. Through him and some internet soul searching I have come across a few podcasts and YouTube channels that I have gained quite a bit from. I look forward to the day that I can fully understand everything they are talking about!
